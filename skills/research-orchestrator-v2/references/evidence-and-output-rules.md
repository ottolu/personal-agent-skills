# Evidence and output rules

## Source tiers

- **Tier 1**: official docs, papers, repos, source code, vendor documentation, filings, benchmarks, launch materials
- **Tier 2**: credible technical blogs, serious industry analysis, high-quality media coverage, expert writeups
- **Tier 3**: forums, social posts, community discussion, anecdotal operator reports

## Evidence rules

- anchor important factual claims in Tier 1 whenever possible
- use Tier 2 to add interpretation, comparison, and context
- use Tier 3 for sentiment, practitioner signal, and hypothesis generation
- do not let weak anecdotal evidence silently upgrade into fact
- explicitly call out stale or conflicting evidence

## Red-team checklist

For serious research, actively check for:
- contradictory claims
- marketing inflation
- missing baselines
- benchmark quality problems
- low-quality adoption evidence
- implementation or deployment friction
- timeline confusion or outdated artifacts

## Output discipline

In the final answer, keep these separate when useful:
- **facts**
- **inferences**
- **judgments**
- **confidence**

Prefer decision-useful structure over note dumps.

## Minimum acceptable synthesis

A serious memo should usually include:
- executive summary
- scope / framing
- key findings
- strongest evidence
- disagreements / uncertainty
- implications / recommendation
- next-step research lanes
